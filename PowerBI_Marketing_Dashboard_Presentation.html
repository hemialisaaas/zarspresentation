About Power BI

Definition

Power BI is Microsoft's Business Intelligence (BI) platform used to collect, process, analyze, and visualize data from multiple sources into interactive reports and dashboards — enabling faster, data-driven decision-making across all business functions.

Core Components

🔗
Power Query
Data extraction, transformation & loading (ETL)
🧮
DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
Custom calculations, KPIs, and measures
📈
Power BI Service
Cloud publishing, sharing & automated refresh

⚡
Speed Up Reporting

Reduce manual report creation from hours to seconds

🔄

📊
Enable Decisions

Provide real-time insights to executives & managers

Section 04

Background — Why Build a Power BI Dashboard?

The marketing team was managing product performance, pricing, and trade data entirely through manual Excel reports — a process that was slow, error-prone, and unable to support real-time business decisions.

📋
Slow
Manual Reporting

Reports took hours to compile each week from scattered Excel files

⚠️
High
Human Error Risk

Manual copy-paste operations introduced inconsistencies and mistakes

🔍
Zero
Real-Time Visibility

No live view of pricing, volume, or trade flows for rapid decisions

📂
Silo
Data Fragmentation

Data lived in separate files per person, with no single source of truth

💡
The Solution: Build automated Power BI dashboards that centralize data, eliminate manual effort, enable real-time monitoring, and empower every stakeholder with self-service analytics.
Section 05 · New

SMART Process in Dashboard Development

S — Specific
Defined Purpose

Dashboard scope is clearly defined: monitor PTA/PX performance, EXIM trade flows, and daily pricing movements — not generic reporting

S
M — Measurable
Clear KPIs

Every dashboard has quantifiable metrics: Value (USD), Quantity (KG), MoM growth %, shipment count, price delta — all tracked over time

M
A — Achievable
Data Ready

All data already exists in structured Excel files; Power Query connects and transforms it into a unified model — no new system required

A
R — Relevant
Business-Driven

Directly supports marketing, sales, and operations decisions: pricing strategy, supply planning, and trade optimization — not vanity metrics

R
T — Time-Bound
Auto-Refresh

Dashboards are configured with scheduled daily/monthly refresh — ensuring data is always current without manual updates

T
Key Insight: Applying SMART ensured that dashboards were built with clear business intent — not just aesthetically appealing visuals, but truly decision-enabling tools.

Section 07C · Dashboard Deep-Dive

Daily Pricing Monitor Dashboard

💹 Objective

Track daily market price movements for PTA and PX, enabling the commercial team to react swiftly to price fluctuations, benchmark against historical trends, and adjust pricing strategies before competitors.

⚡ Why This Matters

Petrochemical prices are highly volatile and influenced by crude oil, currency rates, and global demand. Without a real-time pricing view, teams risk quoting stale prices — losing margin or missing deals. This dashboard solves that directly.

🖥️ Key Features
▶ Daily price trend line (PTA & PX — last 30/90 days)
▶ Day-over-day price change (△ USD/MT with color coding)
▶ Historical average comparison band
▶ Price alert indicators (above/below threshold)
▶ Rolling 7-day / 30-day moving average
💼 Business Value

Transforms a daily manual price-check process into a single-click view — ensuring sales, procurement, and marketing teams are always working from the latest market data, reducing pricing risk and improving response time.

Section 10

Challenge & Response

⚠️ Challenges Faced

📋
Inconsistent Data Formats

Different teams used different date formats, column naming conventions, and unit representations across Excel files, making consolidation impossible without manual fixing.

🕐
Manual Reporting Inefficiency

Weekly performance reports required 2–4 hours of manual compilation — prone to errors and always one step behind current business reality.

🔁
Data Duplication & Silos

Multiple versions of the same data existed across departments, causing confusion and contradicting reports — leading to distrust in numbers.

✅ How We Responded

📐
Standardized Excel Input Templates

Designed locked input templates with data validation rules, standardized column headers, and enforced date/unit formats — eliminating format inconsistency at the source.

⚡
Automated Power BI Dashboards

Replaced manual Excel reports with automated dashboards that refresh daily — reducing reporting effort from hours to zero while improving accuracy and timeliness.

🔧
Power Query Data Cleaning Logic

Built reusable Power Query scripts to detect and flag duplicates, normalize formats, and merge data from all sources into a single verified master dataset.

Section 12

Recommendations — Next Steps

🔌
Integrate Live Data Sources

Connect Power BI directly to ERP/SAP or database systems to eliminate the manual Excel upload step entirely — enabling true real-time data with zero latency.

📱
Mobile Dashboard Access

Publish dashboards to Power BI Mobile so executives can access key metrics anytime, anywhere — ensuring critical decisions are never delayed by being out of office.

🔔
Automated Alerts & Notifications

Set up Power BI data-driven alerts to automatically notify stakeholders when pricing, volume, or KPIs breach defined thresholds — enabling proactive response.

🤖
AI-Powered Forecasting

Leverage Power BI's built-in AI features (Q&A, Key Influencers, Decomposition Tree) or integrate Python/R scripts to provide predictive pricing and demand forecasting.

📚
Data Literacy & Training Program

Roll out a structured training program across marketing and operations — ensuring all users can independently navigate dashboards, apply filters, and extract insights without analyst dependency.

🎯 Final Goal

Build a fully self-service analytics culture where every team member can answer their own data questions — making the organization faster, smarter, and more competitive.
